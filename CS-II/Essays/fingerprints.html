   HTTPS is a secure connection for websites that have the purposes of ensuring a
user's security and privacy. HTTPS makes it harder and more limited for people such
as the FBI and schools to monitor the activity of users on the internet. In order to get
around that they use devices known as HTTPS Proxy Appliances which are a way for
people such as the FBI to look into people's activity on the internet.
   MITM is a man in the middle attack. It is a term used for a perpetrator positions
themselves in a conversation between a user and an application. They do this either to
eavesdrop or to impersonate one of the parties making the situation seem like a normal
exchange. It is risky because whatever information is obtained by the perpetrator could
be used for ill-purposes like identity theft.
   A Hash is a complex mathematical algorithm which carefully processes every
single bit of what they digest The essential qualities that make a has a
good hash is that it has sensitive and strong detection of anything changed
anywhere in a security certificate. In the case of one bit inside the certificate being
changed half of the fingerprint's hash bits will change as well.

   The SSL interception can not be prevented but it can be detected because it is
not possible to completely imitate any security certificate. Public and private keys form
cryptographically matched pairs. The SSL security certificates provide the site's Public
key to the public whereas the private one is never disclosed. The interception does not
have the private key and they may be able to fake one but it wont match, so the
interception is able to be detected.
   A false-positive is when massive companies distribute multiple security
certificates to different servers and websites globally the comparison of the certificates
may lead people to thinking that their connection was intercepted. However they have
actually just received a different certificate. A false negative is when their connection
was intercepted but was not alerted.
